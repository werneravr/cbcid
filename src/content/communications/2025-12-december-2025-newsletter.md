## December 2025

### Murder Incident Update

We regret to report a murder in **Francolin Road** that has deeply affected long-standing, active members of our community, as well as our own staff. Unfortunately we are heavily restricted in what we are permitted to report due to the ongoing SAPS investigation. However, based on the information currently available, it is **not believed to have been a random act** or related to any of the crime syndicates active in the area, and there is **no indication of any ongoing threat to the community**.

Camps Bay CID staff were the **first to arrive** following the call for help when the incident was discovered. Guided by the expertise of our **Public Safety Operations Manager**, the scene was immediately secured to preserve all evidence for SAPS. Support was provided to those who discovered the tragic event, with a **CSO medic** attending to assist during this process. Our teams remained on site to support **SAPS and Forensic Services**, including managing foot and vehicle traffic away from the secured area during the **six-plus hours** required to fully process the scene.

At the same time, our Crime Support Team (Cammy and Esma-eel) began identifying all relevant CCTV footage in the surrounding area, acting under the instruction of senior SAPS officials. This work continued around the clock, including door-to-door visits where necessary, to prevent any footage from being overwritten.

The team, together with Control Room staff, has dedicated many hours to reviewing extensive footage in order to identify all vehicles and persons of interest for investigators. Significant progress has been made in consolidating the most critical information and ensuring a swift and properly documented handover, with the chain of evidence carefully maintained.

Our thoughts are with all those affected by this tragic event. We hope that the commitment and hard work of our staff will assist in bringing those responsible to justice.

## Arrested – "Eskom Crew" Suspects

On **28 December**, an emergency call was received by our Control Room from a resident who observed several males forcing entry into an apartment in the building where she lives. After locking herself in a room, she immediately called for help, triggering an alert to all security and police services in the area regarding an **active crime in progress**. Our team was first to arrive at the address, and a resident assisted them in gaining access to the building.

Upon locating the apartment within the complex and entering through the door that had already been forced open, the officers were **immediately attacked with weapons** by three individuals inside the residence. After a brief altercation and an attempted escape by the suspects, **Officers Poole and Petersen successfully subdued all three**. We thank Bay Response for their swift support on scene, as well as SAPS, who arrived shortly thereafter and arrested the suspects.

![Three Eskom Crew suspects subdued after attacking CID officers](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-eskom-crew-arrested_2cff2fd0.jpg)We are confident that these suspects form part of a group known as the **"Eskom Crew / Roof Rats"**, which has targeted properties in the area for several years and is active across the Cape Town metropolitan area. The suspects have since appeared in court for a bail hearing, with residents attending to demonstrate opposition to their release prior to trial. The hearing has been postponed several times as the legal process continues, and we encourage residents to keep attending.

If you would like to be kept informed of upcoming court dates, times, and venues, please email [**connect@campsbaycid.org**](mailto:connect@campsbaycid.org).

### Festive Season Operations & Community Update

As we move through the busy festive season, the Camps Bay City Improvement District (CBCID) has stepped up operations to help keep Camps Bay safe, clean, and welcoming for everyone who lives, works, and visits here. Below is a snapshot of what's been happening and where our focus has been.

Festive Season Public Safety

To meet the increased activity over this period, our public safety teams have been working extended hours and increasing their presence across the suburb. This includes:

- More foot, vehicle, and beachfront patrols

Extended coverage on peak festive days and nights

- Close coordination with SAPS, Metro Police, Law Enforcement, and City departments

- Highly visible officers acting as deterrents and first responders

These efforts are focused on prevention, quick response, and supporting safe enjoyment of our shared spaces.

Cleaning & Urban Maintenance

Our cleaning and maintenance teams have been working hard to keep Camps Bay looking its best during the festive season:

- **2,799 bags of litter** collected from streets, beaches, and public spaces

- Beachfront areas maintained to high standards

- Street sweeping and debris removal on priority routes

- Green space maintenance and seasonal landscaping

![Camps Bay CID cleaning team in yellow uniforms](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-cleaning-team_2ba5c0c7.jpg)![CID cleaner collecting litter bags on the beach at sunset](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-cleaning-beach-bags_71bab912.jpg)![CID cleaner picking up litter from the beach sand](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-cleaning-beach-litter_81916a7a.jpg)Traffic Management

During peak festive periods, traffic congestion is a significant challenge. Our teams have implemented several measures to improve flow and safety:

- Providing point duty support at key intersections

- Placement of cones on existing red lines in feeder streets to prevent illegal stopping and parking

- Keeping pressure-release roads joining Victoria Road clear and accessible

- Actively addressing illegal parking and obstructions

These measures helped keep traffic moving during peak times and we will use the feedback from the community in order to continue making improvements.

![CID traffic management officer on duty with cones on the road](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-traffic-management-officer_c79c960b.jpg)![CID officer managing traffic with red line cones in a residential street](https://d2xsxph8kpxj0f.cloudfront.net/310519663382560453/YLt7AD2hnj8CinfXJXi2tG/dec2025-traffic-cones-red-lines_3635a85b.jpg)New Water Bowser & Public Space Care

We've added a new water bowser to support urban maintenance and environmental care:

- Used to irrigate public green spaces, reducing reliance on municipal water

- Enables high-pressure cleaning of heavily used public areas

- Can assist as a first-response measure for small fires in emergencies

We continue to welcome assistance from residents with boreholes, which greatly helps conserve potable water.
